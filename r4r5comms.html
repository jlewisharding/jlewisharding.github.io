Family Matters

Mia LFG Famiglia,

When I launched this blog I was announcing the launch of our new identity of LFG. It was a melding of Let’s *$&@!^ Go and the Italian word for family. It was meant to express the two core parts of what I wanted our identity to be.

It is time to revisit this conversation.

We are half a year into life as LFG and something realities have emerged that have given rise to the need for some difficult conversations and descisions.

First, let me be clear that Family before Famiglia will always be our core principal. But we need to clarify what that means. Family before Famiglia means that your real-life (IRL family, friends, and work) responsibilities will always be respected above any expectations for the game.

We need to have some serious talk, though, about the difference between real-life responsibilities and real-life choices and preferences. For some of us, this is a competitive game. I am not athletic (and I’m old and fat now), so I don’t play rec league basketball or church league softball. This game is my rec league. And I like being the player-coach. And I like being competitive about it. And there are about 20-30 other LFG members who behave the same. But the overwhelming majority of the alliance doesn’t participate at the same level. The hard truth is that it is holding us back in some ways and making it harder in others. It’s time we address this.

Actives and Passives

Since the launch, we have focused so much on the LFG side of things and on being a family. But we aren’t LFG: The Family. We are LFG: La Famiglia. If you’ll pardon the inappropriate stereotype, you need to think of our family less like the Cleavers and more like the Corleones.

In other words, the R4s and I-along with about 20-30 of the rest of you-treat LFG like a family business. We are committed to it. We put time (and money for some of us) into this game. We play constantly or off and on throughout the day. We log in for almost all of the events. We are active in the chats. We are responsive. We read announcements and do what they say.

We also carry and protect many of you through major events. And whether you understand it or not, even our most passive players receive benefits that make their casual gameplay better. Here’s an example.

I recently posted a screenshot of my progress on researching the tech for level 10 (T10) troops. The final 4 research techs all will cost me more than half a BILLION gold each. And I have spent BILLIONS and BILLIONS of gold on the ones before those.

Now I want you to look at the Alliance Tech. We just finished the Level 10 Senior Scientist, which makes all research cost 5% less. Now, 5% may not sound like a lot, but 5% of 500 million gold is 25 million. As far as I know, only a handful of us are close to completing the T10 research, but we got here without that same discount. But if you haven’t started yet, then when you do it, it will cost you probably several hundred million less gold than it cost me and the others.

That discount was bought with alliance tech donations that probably came from the most active people in the alliance. But you get the buff, along with dozens others. And don’t forget about the cities and dig sites we have captured this season. Look at the image here and see how many seasonal buffs you get right now just by having LFGo as part of your account. Now many of you have helped with the capturing of these cities. But most of you don’t.

A Family Business or A Family Dinner

We are a family here. But we are going to start making some distinctions. Some of you are part of the family business, you’re members of our organized crime syndicate. You put in the work day in and day out. But some (maybe most) of LFG just comes for family dinner and lets the rest of us feed them.

That being said, we aren’t going to start booting anyone (for now). There are two reasons why.

  1. You are still family, whether you are active or not. That means something to me. I know that some of you have special circumstances that make it hard for you to play regularly or as much as the upper 30% of actives.

  2. There’s no real benefit to booting family members who show up just to eat dinner. There’s not a long line of people in the server just waiting for their chance to join.

Moving people off the payroll

The hard truth is that something needs to be done. Soon the R4s and I will start demoting many of you to R1 status. Moving forward, this means that you will have a safe space here. You will be protected and you will still get our buffs. But here’s the other side of that.

  • No one in LFG will be expected to carry you through monsters (First Blood Doom Walkers that are way too strong for you) or events (like General’s Trial) that are way too difficult.
  • We will ask that everyone prioritize assisting Ghost Ops and Secret tasks that belong to R2-R5. It is not ok for someone who logs in and participates regularly to miss out on a UR ghost ops task because one of our more rare 4-star heroes (e.g., Lucius or Adam) is tied up on a ghost op for someone who logs in once a day and rarely participates. It also bothers me when I see UR tasks from committed members at risk of being plundered and no one has assists left because they used them on people who are not active.
  • If Enemy Buster is not important for a weekly victory, we will not reinforce your base when you don’t shield. We won’t be burning troops for our active members to try and protect our most casual people.

Tough Love

Fam, I love you all. I have prayed for many of you. Sent gift cards to some of you when tough things have happened in your life. I have given my personal cell number out to multiples of you. I have spent hours of my life-outside of playing this game-lobbying in Discord for things that will help you enjoy the game better when you log in. At the same time, I have been and continue to be asked by some of the bigger alliances if I want to join them. I could be getting way better rewards and growing my account far faster and for a lot fewer US dollars elsewhere. But I love what we’ve built here. I am committed to La FamiGlia. I have thrown a lot of time and energy and money into growing my account so I can better protect each of you. So have many others in LFG. But there is an expectation that you are also committed to the family. We can be a family of takers and givers, but I don’t want that to be who we are.

If you are concerned about your position in all of this, just reach out to me or another R4. We don’t have a timeline yet for this, but it will likely begin either the first of the year or after season 2 ends.

I know the holidays are still in full swing. I’m not changing my position that says expectations will be relaxed. But many of the less active folks aren’t less active than they normally are right now. They are as they have always been. We know who these folks are. And the ones who are unusually busy have reached out for the most part.

I hope that you are reading this and rather than feeling worried, you are stirred up a bit to either continue your current high level activity or feel encouraged to step it up.

Either way…

There will be some urgency moving forward because other alliances are trying to kill the stronghold guardians to farm the professional expertise.
